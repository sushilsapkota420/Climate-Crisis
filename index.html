The Wildfire Crisis

Understanding why wildfires are increasing, how they affect people and wildlife, and what we can do to help.

Why Are Wildfires Increasing?

Hotter Temperatures

Climate change causes longer heat waves and drier conditions, making forests easier to burn.

Drought

Less rainfall dries out trees, grass, and soil, creating fuel for large fires.

Human Activity

Campfires, power lines, and careless behavior can start many wildfires.

The Impacts of Wildfires

Air Pollution

Smoke from wildfires can make the air unhealthy and dangerous to breathe.

Destroyed Homes

Wildfires can spread quickly and damage neighborhoods, roads, and communities.

Wildlife Loss

Animals lose shelter, food, and safe habitats during major fires.

More Carbon Emissions

Burning forests release carbon dioxide, which can worsen climate change.

How Can We Prevent Wildfires?

Forest Management

Clearing dead plants and reducing dry brush can lower fire risk.

Controlled Burns

Small planned fires can safely remove fuel before dangerous fires begin.

Climate Action

Using cleaner energy and lowering emissions can reduce extreme heat and drought.

Quick Facts

  • Wildfires can spread faster in hot, dry, and windy weather.
  • Many wildfires are started by human activity.
  • Smoke can travel long distances and affect cities far away.
  • Wildfires damage forests, homes, and animal habitats.
  • Climate change increases heat and drought risk.
  • Prevention and preparation can reduce wildfire damage.
